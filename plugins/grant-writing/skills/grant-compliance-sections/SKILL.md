# Compliance and Rigor Sections

These sections are graded against fixed regulatory criteria, not persuasive quality — a Vertebrate Animals Section that omits one of its required points is marked incomplete regardless of how well it reads. Precision and completeness are the goal.

Read `00_admin/project-config.md` and the Approach. Output to `07_compliance/`, versioned. Regulatory formats change; verify each against current agency instructions and the FOA, and route substantive compliance questions to the user's IRB/IACUC and grants office — the model drafts and checks completeness, it does not adjudicate compliance.

## Human Subjects

If the project involves human subjects or their identifiable data/specimens:

- **Determination first**: confirm with the user whether the work is human-subjects research and whether it meets the clinical-trial definition (NIH's four questions) — this drives which FOA is even eligible and which forms apply.
- **PHS Human Subjects and Clinical Trials Information**: per study — protection of subjects (risks, protections, risk/benefit), data and safety monitoring for trials.
- **Inclusion**: inclusion of women, minorities, and individuals across the lifespan; inclusion enrollment reports; scientific justification for any exclusion (age, sex/gender) — blanket exclusions need defensible rationale.
- Consistency with the recruitment numbers in the Approach, timeline, and budget.

## Vertebrate Animals Section (VAS)

Must address all required points, or it's flagged incomplete: (1) description of procedures and species/strains/numbers with scientific justification for the numbers; (2) justification for using animals, appropriateness of species and numbers; (3) minimization of pain and distress, analgesia/anesthesia. Note euthanasia consistency with AVMA guidelines. Animal numbers must match the budget and power analysis.

## Rigor and Reproducibility

Often woven into the Approach but sometimes a distinct expectation. Ensure the proposal addresses: scientific premise rigor (strengths/weaknesses of the supporting literature — state candidly), rigorous experimental design (controls, blinding, randomization, sample-size justification), relevant biological variables, and authentication.

**Sex as a Biological Variable (SABV)**: NIH expects sex factored into design/analysis for vertebrate animal and human studies, or strong justification for studying one sex. Check the Approach actually does this; a boilerplate SABV sentence contradicted by a single-sex design is a common reviewer catch.

**Authentication of Key Biological and/or Chemical Resources**: separate NIH attachment — how key resources (cell lines, antibodies, specialty chemicals, transgenic organisms) will be authenticated. Not the methods, just the authentication approach. Cell lines should be checked against ICLAC misidentified-lines lists; note mycoplasma/STR testing where relevant.

## Biohazards and Select Agents

If applicable: biosafety level, IBC oversight, select-agent registration status. Brief and factual.

## Cross-checks

Every compliance section's numbers (subjects, animals) reconcile with budget, timeline, and Approach. Report any mismatch and any required element left blank as a blocking issue — these stop applications at the submission-validation stage.
