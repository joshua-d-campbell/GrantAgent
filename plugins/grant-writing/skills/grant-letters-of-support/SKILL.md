# Letters of Support

Letters must be drafted early: collaborators revise slowly, travel, and miss deadlines. The practical workflow is that the PI's team drafts every letter and the signer edits and signs. Target having drafts out at least 4–6 weeks before the deadline.

Read `00_admin/project-config.md` for the team list and deadline. Letters live in `05_support_letters/`, one file per signer, versioned; track status in `05_support_letters/letter-tracker.md` (signer, role, draft sent, received signed, format OK).

## Step 1 — Build the letter list

From the aims, budget, and approach, enumerate who must attest to what:

- Collaborators contributing expertise, data, reagents, or site access
- Consultants (with role and, if paid, consistency with the budget)
- Core facilities / shared instruments (capacity and access commitments)
- Subaward site leads (in addition to formal subaward paperwork)
- For DoD/clinical work: patient advocates, military treatment facilities, data providers as the FOA requires

Flag gaps factually: any resource or expertise claimed in the Approach without a letter, and any letter without a corresponding narrative mention.

## Step 2 — Draft each letter

Structure (adapt to signer's voice, ~1 page, signer's letterhead):

1. Addressed to the PI (not "To whom it may concern" if avoidable); references the specific proposal title and mechanism.
2. Who the signer is and their relevant standing — one or two sentences.
3. The specific commitment: what they will provide (expertise, samples, instrument hours, data access, meeting cadence), stated concretely. Generic enthusiasm letters ("I fully support this exciting project") carry no weight with reviewers; specific commitments do.
4. Brief statement of the collaboration's history or fit, where true.
5. Closing commitment sentence.

Write in the signer's plausible voice, not the PI's. Vary structure and phrasing across letters — reviewers notice a stack of letters with identical bones.

## Agency constraints (verify against FOA)

- **NSF**: letters of *collaboration* are restricted to a bare commitment statement (the PAPPG single-sentence format); endorsement language can get a proposal returned without review. Check the solicitation before drafting anything richer.
- **NIH**: letters of support are expected for consultants, collaborators, core access; combine into a single PDF attachment per instructions; keep dated within the application period.
- **DoD/foundations**: per announcement; CDMRP often requires specific letters (e.g., military relevance collaborators).

## Step 3 — Send, track, chase

Produce a short request email per signer (what's attached, what to check, deadline for return — set it 2+ weeks before the real deadline). Update the tracker as letters return; verify each signed letter's date, letterhead, and signature before filing to `09_final_assembly/`.
