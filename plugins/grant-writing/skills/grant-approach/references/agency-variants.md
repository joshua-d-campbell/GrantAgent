# Approach Variants by Funder

Specific solicitations override guide defaults (page counts, required headings, SOW templates). Verify structure against the FOA/NOFO/BAA and the current agency guide before committing to an outline.

## NIH
The per-aim template in SKILL.md is the NIH default. Factor 2 (Rigor and Feasibility) scoring means reviewers explicitly look for: scientific rigor of design, appropriate statistics and power, consideration of relevant biological variables (including sex), authentication of key resources (separate attachment), and feasibility given preliminary data and timeline. Human subjects and vertebrate animal detail goes in dedicated forms/sections, not the Approach — but the design summary in the Approach must be consistent with them.

## NSF
The 15-page Project Description has no mandated internal structure except: Broader Impacts must appear as a separately labeled section, and Results from Prior NSF Support (up to 5 pages within the 15) is required for any senior person with NSF funding in the past 5 years — covering both Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts of the prior award. A common structure: Introduction/Objectives → Background → Research Plan by objective (rationale, methods, expected results, contingencies) → Broader Impacts → Timeline/Management. Education/outreach plans need the same rigor as the science: named partners, assessment plan. Reviewers rate both merit criteria through the same standard elements (potential to advance knowledge / benefit society; creative and original concepts; sound and well-organized plan; qualified team; adequate resources) — structure the plan so each is directly answerable, and check the specific solicitation for added criteria.

## DoD
Organize around the Statement of Work when one is required: numbered tasks and subtasks, each with entrance/exit criteria, deliverables, and schedule. Many BAAs want a risk matrix (technical risks × likelihood × mitigation). For DARPA-style proposals, structure the technical approach to answer the Heilmeier catechism: What are you trying to do? How is it done today and what are the limits? What is new and why will it succeed? Who cares / what difference will it make? What are the risks? How much will it cost and how long? What are the mid-term and final exams (metrics)? Quantitative metrics for each phase are expected, not optional.

CDMRP is its own culture within DoD: military relevance (or the program's disease focus) is a scored criterion and should be woven through the research strategy, not confined to one paragraph. Program announcements frequently supply mandatory templates (SOW, milestones) — use them verbatim; deviation is a compliance problem, not a style choice. Budget extra calendar time for HRPO/ACURO second-level review of human and animal work when planning the period of performance.

## Foundations
Approach sections are shorter and less methods-dense; feasibility is often argued through team track record rather than protocol detail. Keep a full-rigor internal version — many foundations request detail at a later stage, and the internal version feeds progress reports.
