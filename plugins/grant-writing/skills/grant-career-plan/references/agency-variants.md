# Career-Award Variants by Funder

Verify everything below against the specific FOA/PA/solicitation before relying on it. Facts current as of July 2026 where checkable; NIH has been migrating mechanisms to revised review frameworks (research grants Jan 2025, fellowships 2025), so **confirm the current K-award review instrument in the PA** rather than assuming the classic criteria.

## NIH K series (mentored career development)

**Mechanisms** (mentored; the FOA governs): K01 (research scientist), K08 (clinician, lab-based), K23 (clinician, patient-oriented), K25 (quantitative scientist crossing into biomedicine), K99/R00 (postdoc-to-independence, two-phase). Most mentored K's require US citizenship or permanent residency; **K99 does not** — it is the main NIH career mechanism open to non-citizens.

**Review criteria** (classic instrument — confirm currency in the PA): five scored criteria — Candidate; Career Development Plan / Career Goals & Objectives; Research Plan; Mentor(s), Co-Mentor(s), Consultant(s), Collaborator(s); Environment & Institutional Commitment to the Candidate — plus Overall Impact framed as the likelihood the award will *enhance the candidate's potential for an independent research career*, not just the science's merit.

**Effort**: typically a minimum of 75% professional effort (9 person-months) devoted to the award, with some clinician mechanisms/specialties permitted 50% — the FOA states which. The institutional letter must commit the percentage explicitly and describe how clinical/teaching load is reduced to make it real.

**Reference letters**: 3–5, submitted by the referees themselves through eRA Commons by the deadline — not by the applicant, and not from mentors, co-mentors, or anyone named in the application. An application can be rejected for missing letters. Longest-lead item in the application; brief referees early with a summary of the candidate's plan.

**K99/R00 specifics**: eligibility window is limited postdoctoral experience at time of application (currently no more than 4 years — verify, it has changed before); the K99 phase (≤2 years, mentored) must justify what training remains, and the R00 phase requires a tenure-track-equivalent independent position to activate. Aims must partition cleanly: mentored-phase aims executable in the mentor's lab, independent-phase aims portable to the candidate's future lab — reviewers specifically probe whether the R00 work depends on staying put.

**RCR**: instruction plan described across format, subject matter, faculty participation, duration, and frequency; at least eight face-to-face contact hours is the working standard, online-only insufficient, refreshed at each career stage.

## NSF CAREER

Solicitation **NSF 22-586** (verify current; deadline the fourth Wednesday in July, annually). Single PI, no co-PIs, one CAREER proposal per year, and a lifetime cap on the number of CAREER competitions a PI may enter (three under recent solicitations — verify).

**Not a mentored award** — no mentor team, no referee letters. The distinctive requirement is an **integrated research and education plan**, judged under the standard Intellectual Merit / Broader Impacts criteria: education activities must be integral to the research, assessable, and scaled to the PI — a bolted-on outreach paragraph is the classic CAREER kill. The career-development narrative is the PI's own path to leadership in integrating the two.

**Departmental letter**: one letter from the department chair (or equivalent) with solicitation-prescribed content — support for the PI's integrated plan and its relation to the department's goals, and the PI's eligibility; it is a compliance item, and content beyond what the solicitation asks (e.g., endorsement language) risks return without review. This is the CAREER analog of institutional commitment.

PECASE nominees are selected from among CAREER awardees; there is no separate application.

## Foundation career awards

Examples: Burroughs Wellcome, Pew, Searle, Damon Runyon, ACS and AHA career development awards. Almost all require a mentor or department-head statement and institutional commitment; criteria come from the RFP and often include mission fit and a lay summary for board readers (see the foundations note in `grant-mock-review`'s criteria file). Two structural traps: many are **nomination-limited** (the institution may put forward only 1–2 candidates, so an internal selection round precedes the real deadline — find that internal date first), and eligibility windows are counted from degree or faculty-start date with little flexibility. Confirm both before any drafting effort.
