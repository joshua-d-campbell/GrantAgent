# Facilities, Equipment, and Resources

These documents establish that the environment can support the work — Factor 3 material at NIH. They are boilerplate-prone; the useful version is project-specific: every listed resource should map to something the Approach actually needs.

Read `00_admin/project-config.md` and the Approach drafts. Files go to `07_compliance/` (or a dedicated subfolder), versioned. Collect the institution's existing facilities boilerplate from the user — most departments maintain one — and treat it as raw material, not the answer.

## Facilities & Other Resources

Organize by category, describing each only as it serves this project:

- **Laboratory**: space (sq ft where notable), location relative to collaborators/cores, specialized rooms (BSL levels, clean rooms, animal housing adjacency)
- **Core facilities**: each core the Approach uses — instrumentation, staffing, access terms; consistency check against letters of support and budget recharge lines
- **Computing/data**: clusters, storage, HIPAA/secure environments where human data are involved (must match the DMSP)
- **Clinical**: patient populations, recruitment infrastructure, registries — with the numbers that make the recruitment plan credible
- **Office/administrative**, and **institutional environment**: departmental strengths, relevant centers, institutional commitment (startup, protected time) where real
- NIH asks how the environment contributes to probability of success — one synthesis paragraph, factual

For early-stage investigators, NIH invites description of institutional investment (mentoring, protected time, startup); include when applicable.

## Equipment

List major items available to the project (typically ≥$5K per unit, but the criterion is relevance): instrument, model where it matters to capability claims, location/ownership (lab vs core). Items the budget requests to *purchase* do not belong here — that's the budget; equipment lists what already exists. A requested instrument that duplicates a listed one is a reviewer objection; reconcile before submission.

## Cross-checks

Every method in the Approach has its enabling resource listed here; every resource claimed here that belongs to someone else has a letter of support; DMSP storage claims match the computing description. Report mismatches factually. Where the institutional boilerplate claims resources the user cannot actually access, cut them — reviewers at the same institution notice.
